A living showcase of biomedical engineering.

In high school, I dreamt of becoming a medical doctor, saving lives, and making a difference in a hospital setting. Life had other plans.

I graduated (BSc & MSc) in Biomedical Engineering from Instituto Superior Técnico (IST), specializing in imaging, biosignals, and biomedical instrumentation.

Side-quested alone for 6 months at Danmarks Tekniske Universitet (DTU) in Copenhagen, Denmark, as part of the Erasmus Programme.

To further explore my Master’s Thesis, I received two research grants from FCT to study the role of cytoskeletons and nuclei in cancer cells at the Institute for Systems and Robotics (ISR-Lisboa).

Fell in love with my work and was awarded a PhD fellowship from FCT to pursue my research interests at IST/ISR.

Along the way, I joined outreach and dissemination activities promoted by IST and ISR, contributed to teaching in the Biomedical Engineering BSc at IST, and attended several national and international meetings.

Right now, I’m a visiting researcher at the Wirtz / Wu Lab at Johns Hopkins University (JHU) in Baltimore, USA.
